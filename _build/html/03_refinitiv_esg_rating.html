Refinitiv ESG data#

Refinitiv’s description of its methodology is disclosed here.

Summary#

  • covers 85% of market capitalization with its ratings

  • uses 630 indicators

  • data starts at 2002

  • measures relative ESG performance with respect to sector and country

  • Refinitiv claims that “it does not presume to define what good looks like; it lets the data determine industry-based relative performance…”

  • they determine scores for four pillars: environmental, social, governance and controversy; the scores are aggregated to an ESG and an ESGC score

Data#

  • they employ about 700 analysts to collect ESG data

  • they collect data from publicly available sources

from IPython.display import Image
Image(filename='refinitiv_data_sources.jpg', width = 100)

  • the 630 indicators are manually processed by analysts

  • the goal is to standardize each measure for comparability among all companies

  • data is updated regularly on a yearly basis, controversies are included “on the fly”

  • currently 12,500 companies with an emphasis on North America (3800), Asia (3100) and Europe (2500)

ESG scores#

  • a subset of 186 ESG measures is used

  • these measures are grouped into 10 categories:

    • Environmental: ressource use, emissions, innovation

    • Social: workforce, human rights, community, product responsibility

    • Governance: management, shareholders, csr strategy

  • a score is determined for each category

  • each ESG pillar score is a relative sum of category weights which vary per industry for the environmental and the social pillar

  • the ESGC score is accompanied by significant media controversies of the company and is supposed to discount the ESG score in case of large controversies

  • 23 controversy topics exist which are aggregated to a controversy score

  • Boolean ESG indicators are represented by 1 and 0, while 0 is the default value even if no information is available w.r.t. this indicator; numeric indicator variables are only used as reported and enter the scoring by a industry relative ranking; all indicators have a polarity which indicates if high values are positive or negative

  • A ranking score for each of the 10 categories is determined by:

\[ \text{score} = \frac{\text{no. of companies with a worse value} + \frac{\text{no. of companies with the same value}}{2}}{\text{no. of companies with a value}} \]

  • The enironmental and social scores are determined on TRBC industry group level

  • The governance score is determined based on country level

  • Materiality of themes for each of the 10 categories and each industry is approximated by sufficient disclosure of data w.r.t. to the theme

  • Category weights are determined based on materiality, see the documentation for more details
